# Product Vision

## Problem Statement

Healthcare operational teams need early, explainable signals about admissions that may require longer inpatient stays so they can plan flow, discharge coordination, and service capacity. The future product will use synthetic data to demonstrate how those signals can be delivered safely through a governed analytical-product lifecycle.

## Vision

The product vision is a reproducible decision-support platform that connects governed logical data access, Python machine learning, FastAPI scoring, R-Shiny operational workflows, monitoring, and controlled retraining. The repository is designed to demonstrate product delivery practices rather than a one-off notebook model.

## Target Users

Target users include operational flow managers, discharge-planning users, performance analysts, data scientists, data engineers, R-Shiny developers, QA analysts, information-governance representatives, platform engineers, service-support analysts, and the product owner.

## Intended Decisions

Future users will be able to review synthetic risk signals, prioritise operational follow-up, understand cohort-level demand, provide feedback, and review model-health evidence. The product must support human judgement and accountability.

## Value Proposition

The platform will show how an analytical product can move from requirements to governed data, tested model development, user-facing delivery, monitoring, retraining control, and handover evidence.

## Success Measures

Success will be measured by reproducible validation, clear scope control, documented governance, tested workflows, transparent model evidence, and user-facing workflows that remain honest about limitations.

## Non-Goals

The project is not a clinical tool, not an automated decision-maker, not a live healthcare integration, and not a substitute for local operational policy.

## Ethical and Operational Boundaries

Only synthetic data may be used. The product must avoid real patient data, real credentials, and fabricated commercial-tool evidence. Model promotion will require human approval in later milestones.
