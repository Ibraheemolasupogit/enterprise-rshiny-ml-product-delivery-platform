# User Personas

## Operational Flow Manager

Goals: anticipate demand, coordinate capacity, and review high-risk admissions. Responsibilities: daily operational oversight and escalation. Needs: clear cohort views, explainable risk, and confidence indicators. Pain points: fragmented information and late escalation. Interaction: uses the future R-Shiny application to review synthetic risk lists and feedback.

## Discharge-Planning User

Goals: identify patients who may need earlier discharge planning. Responsibilities: coordinate discharge actions and capture outcomes. Needs: concise patient-level context. Pain points: unclear prioritisation. Interaction: reviews individual synthetic risk records and records feedback in a future workflow.

## Performance Analyst

Goals: understand trends and service performance. Responsibilities: reporting and operational insight. Needs: cohort summaries and reproducible definitions. Pain points: inconsistent extracts. Interaction: uses validated outputs and documentation.

## Data Scientist

Goals: develop, evaluate, and monitor models. Responsibilities: modelling, evidence, and threshold review. Needs: synthetic data contracts, reproducible pipelines, and governance checkpoints. Pain points: unclear promotion rules. Interaction: works in Python lifecycle components added after Milestone 1.

## Data Engineer

Goals: provide reliable governed data access. Responsibilities: source modelling, validation, and lineage. Needs: clear contracts and logical-view design. Pain points: undocumented transformations. Interaction: builds future synthetic source and governed logical data layer components.

## R-Shiny Developer

Goals: build modular operational workflows. Responsibilities: R package structure, Shiny modules, tests, and accessibility. Needs: stable API contracts and user journeys. Pain points: late-changing requirements. Interaction: builds future R-Shiny modules under `rshiny/`.

## QA Analyst

Goals: validate behaviour and prevent regressions. Responsibilities: test strategy, automated checks, UAT support. Needs: acceptance criteria and traceability. Pain points: untestable claims. Interaction: extends Python, R, contract, integration, and end-to-end tests.

## Information-Governance Representative

Goals: ensure safe data handling and transparent governance. Responsibilities: review data boundaries, lineage, retention, and approval evidence. Needs: synthetic-data proof and no-secrets controls. Pain points: informal evidence. Interaction: reviews governance documentation and future promotion evidence.

## Platform Engineer

Goals: enable reliable CI/CD and runtime hosting. Responsibilities: infrastructure, secrets, access control, and release safety. Needs: clean separation between build and deployment. Pain points: undocumented manual steps. Interaction: owns future deployment patterns.

## Service-Support Analyst

Goals: support users and incidents. Responsibilities: triage, runbooks, known issues, and rollback coordination. Needs: operational handover artefacts. Pain points: missing ownership. Interaction: uses future support runbooks and monitoring dashboards.

## Product Owner

Goals: prioritise value and maintain scope. Responsibilities: backlog, acceptance, release approval, and stakeholder alignment. Needs: milestone status and user feedback. Pain points: hidden technical debt. Interaction: owns roadmap and release decisions.
